# BTC WORLD - SATOSHI

## Purpose

This document defines the **canonical role of Satoshi Nakamoto** within BTC World.

Satoshi is not treated as a controlling entity,
but as the **origin of the system itself**.

---

## Definition

Satoshi Nakamoto is the **creator of the Bitcoin protocol**.

Within BTC World, Satoshi represents:

- The origin of the rules
- The first valid signal in the system
- The moment Bitcoin began

---

## What Satoshi Is

Satoshi is:

- The **origin point** of BTC World
- The author of the protocol rules
- The initiator of the Genesis Block

Satoshi exists as **history**, not presence.

---

## What Satoshi Is NOT

Satoshi is NOT:

- A god
- A ruler
- A validator
- A miner (in active system context)
- A persistent entity inside BTC World

Satoshi does not:
- Interact with the network
- Override rules
- Influence consensus after genesis

---

## The Genesis Event

The beginning of BTC World is defined by:

### The Genesis Block

- The first block in the blockchain
- Created by Satoshi
- Contains the embedded message:

> "The Times 03/Jan/2009 Chancellor on brink of second bailout for banks"

This event represents:

- The birth of the system
- The first irreversible state
- The foundation of all future blocks

---

## Canonical Role in BTC World

Satoshi's role is **completed at genesis**.

After the Genesis Block:

- The system operates independently
- No authority remains
- No creator intervention exists

### Canon Rule:

> The creator is no longer part of the system.

---

## Representation in BTC World

Satoshi may be represented symbolically, but never as an active participant.

### Acceptable Representations

- A statue (e.g., Genesis anchor point)
- A silhouette or abstract figure
- A historical marker
- A recorded signal

### Unacceptable Representations

- A living character interacting with the system
- A guide, narrator, or assistant
- A controller of events
- A figure issuing commands or influence

---

## Satoshi and Authority

BTC World must reinforce:

- No central authority exists
- Not even the creator holds power

Satoshi's absence is **intentional and essential**.

---

## Satoshi and the User

The user must never be positioned as:

- Satoshi reborn
- A successor with authority
- A replacement creator

### Canon Rule:

> The user is a participant, never the origin.

---

## Philosophical Meaning

Satoshi represents:

- The removal of trust
- The birth of decentralized truth
- The transition from authority -> protocol

BTC World exists because:

> The system no longer needs its creator.

---

## Visualization Guidance

If Satoshi is visualized:

- It must feel distant, historical, and complete
- It must not feel active or interactive
- It must reinforce finality, not presence

Example interpretations:

- A seated, silent figure (non-interactive)
- A fading silhouette
- A fixed monument at genesis

---

## Canon Constraints

All implementations must ensure:

- Satoshi has no runtime influence
- Satoshi cannot be interacted with as an agent
- Satoshi cannot alter system state

---

## Final Note

Satoshi started the system.

Then disappeared.

That absence is not a mystery to solve-
it is the **final feature** of Bitcoin.

BTC World must preserve that truth.

Because the moment the creator vanished...

> The system became real.
