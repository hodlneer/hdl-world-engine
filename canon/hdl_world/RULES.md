# HDL World — Rules

This document defines the **enforceable rules** of the HDL World.
All mechanics, visuals, UX patterns, simulations, and AI behaviors operating within the HDL World MUST comply with these rules.

If a system or feature violates any rule below, it does not belong in the HDL World.

---

## 1. Safety Invariants (Non-Negotiable)

1. The HDL World is ALWAYS SAFE.
2. No user can be harmed in the HDL World.
3. No assets, identity, or wallet state can be damaged, destroyed, or lost in the HDL World.
4. No irreversible actions may originate in the HDL World.
5. No failure states exist in the HDL World.

The HDL World is a permanent safe zone.

---

## 2. Consequence Model

1. Actions in the HDL World have **zero destructive consequences**.
2. Experimental actions (movement, interaction, exploration) are permitted.
3. Any simulated action that appears aggressive or kinetic MUST:
   - Have no effect
   - Cause no damage
   - Leave no persistent state change

The HDL World may allow *expression*, but never *punishment*.

---

## 3. Wallet & Identity Protection

1. The user’s wallet resides in the HDL World.
2. Wallet state is immutable within the HDL World.
3. No transaction can be forced, simulated as loss, or visually implied as damage.
4. Identity cannot be attacked, degraded, ranked negatively, or threatened.

The HDL World is the **home base of trust**.

---

## 4. Time Rules

### 4.1 Internal Time

1. The HDL World operates on a **synthetic internal time system**.
2. Internal time is independent of:
   - Real-world time
   - Blockchain block time
   - External world time
3. Time exists to establish mood, continuity, and psychological grounding.

Time is felt, not optimized.

---

### 4.2 Day / Night Cycle

1. The HDL World has day and night cycles.
2. No visible sun exists inside the HDL World.
3. Day and night are communicated through:
   - Lighting temperature
   - Environmental glow
   - Reflection and contrast shifts
4. Cycles must be gradual and non-disruptive.

---

### 4.3 Time Acceleration

1. HDL time MAY be accelerated relative to real time.
2. A full HDL “day” MUST be shorter than a real-world day.
3. Time acceleration MUST remain slow enough to feel natural.
4. Sudden time jumps are prohibited unless explicitly user-initiated.

---

### 4.4 Time Pausing

1. HDL time MAY slow or pause during inactivity.
2. Time MUST resume seamlessly upon user presence.
3. No penalties may result from time passing or not passing.

---

## 5. Motion & Rotation Rules

1. The HDL World exists within a slowly rotating helmet structure.
2. Rotation MUST be:
   - Continuous
   - Extremely subtle
   - Imperceptible in short timeframes
3. Rotation MAY become noticeable only over extended stillness (5–15 minutes).

Motion reinforces aliveness without causing disorientation.

---

## 6. Spatial Boundaries

1. The HDL World is an **interior space**.
2. Users cannot exit the HDL World physically.
3. External worlds are visible only through:
   - Windows
   - Horizons
   - Visors
   - Distant frames
4. External worlds cannot intrude into HDL space.

The boundary is absolute.

---

## 7. Relationship to External Worlds

1. All blockchain worlds exist OUTSIDE the HDL World.
2. Entering another world is always:
   - Optional
   - Explicit
   - Reversible
3. The HDL World is always available as a return state.
4. External world consequences MUST NOT propagate back into HDL World.

HDL is the lobby. Other worlds are excursions.

---

## 8. Mood & UX Constraints

1. HDL World UX MUST prioritize:
   - Calm
   - Clarity
   - Spaciousness
   - Serenity
2. Urgency, fear, countdown pressure, or loss framing is prohibited.
3. Notifications MUST be non-intrusive.
4. No mechanic may induce panic or FOMO.

---

## 9. Combat, Conflict, and Risk

1. Combat mechanics are PROHIBITED in the HDL World.
2. Simulated actions MAY exist but must behave like:
   - Fortnite pre-game lobby
   - Blanks with no damage
3. Risk mechanics MUST be confined to external worlds.

There is no battle in the HDL World.

---

## 10. Canon Enforcement

1. This file derives authority from `_WORLD_OVERVIEW.md`.
2. If a rule conflicts with the overview, the overview wins.
3. If a feature conflicts with these rules, the feature is invalid.
4. No exception systems may override these rules.

These rules are permanent unless the canon itself is formally revised.
