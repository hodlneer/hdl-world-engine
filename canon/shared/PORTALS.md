# HDL World Taxonomy

This document defines the structural relationship between HDL and all external worlds.

---

# Layer 1 - HDL (The Center)

HDL is the central world of the entire universe.

It contains:

- the helmet interior
- OD (the governing intelligence)
- the user's wallet identity
- the social layer
- the Twelve Disciples
- navigation portals

HDL is the only world that connects all ecosystems.

Users always begin here.

---

# Layer 2 - Blockchain Worlds

Blockchain ecosystems exist as external worlds.

These include:

- Bitcoin World
- Ethereum World
- XRP World
- Solana World

Each world is independent.

Each world has its own:

- visual language
- entities
- mechanics
- timeline

---

# Bitcoin World

The Bitcoin World represents the Bitcoin ecosystem.

Examples of entities:

- Satoshi Nakamoto statue
- Genesis Block structures
- Mining constructs
- Adoption geography

The Bitcoin World is where Bitcoin-specific symbolism exists.

It does not exist inside HDL.

---

# Ethereum World

The Ethereum World represents programmable blockchain systems.

Potential visualizations include:

- smart contract structures
- gas flows
- DeFi constructs

---

# XRP World

The XRP World represents cross-border payment infrastructure.

Visual metaphors may include:

- liquidity channels
- banking nodes
- settlement networks

---

# Solana World

The Solana World represents high-speed blockchain infrastructure.

Possible representations include:

- high-frequency validator networks
- parallel computation systems

---

# Future Worlds

Additional ecosystems may appear over time.

These may include:

- Layer 2 systems
- DeFi networks
- new blockchain ecosystems

All worlds must connect through HDL.

---

# Portal Behavior

Portals allow travel between HDL and external worlds.

Portal rules:

- portals always originate in HDL
- portals always return to HDL
- portals never connect external worlds directly

Travel always passes through the center.

---

# The Center Always Holds

No matter how many worlds appear:

HDL remains the center.
