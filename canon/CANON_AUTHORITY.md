# HDL Canon --- Authority

This document defines the governance model for how canon is created, ratified, and maintained within the HDL World Engine repository.

The purpose of this file is to ensure that the HDL universe evolves
**deliberately and coherently**, without accidental contradictions or
fragmentation.

It is a meta-canonical document and does not supersede doctrinal authority defined in `CANON_INVARIANTS.md`.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 1. The Repository Is The Source Of Truth

The HDL canon exists **only inside this repository**.

Discussions, conversations, prototypes, simulations, and experiments may
explore ideas, but they do **not become canon** until they are written
into the repository.

If a discussion and the repository disagree:

**The repository wins.**

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 2. Canon Hierarchy

Documents in the HDL canon follow a strict hierarchy of authority.

Higher layers override lower layers.

1.  CANON_INVARIANTS.md
2.  UNIVERSAL_RULES.md
3.  WORLD_OVERVIEW.md
4.  RULES.md
5.  ENTITY FILES

If a conflict occurs between documents, the document at the higher level
takes precedence.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 3. Canon Update Process

Canon evolves through a deliberate process.

Step 1 --- Discovery\
Ideas emerge through exploration, conversation, or design work.

Step 2 --- Proposal\
A canonical update is drafted and evaluated against existing invariants
and rules.

Step 3 --- Integration\
Approved changes are written into the appropriate canon documents.

Step 4 --- Ratification\
Once committed to the repository, the change becomes part of official
canon.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 4. Canon Stability

Canon should change **rarely and intentionally**.

The purpose of the canon system is to prevent:

• conceptual drift\
• contradictory world design\
• accidental symbolism conflicts\
• fragmented interpretations

New worlds, entities, and systems should fit **within existing
doctrine** whenever possible.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 5. World Independence

Each world within the HDL universe maintains its own doctrine and rules.

Examples include:

• HDL World\
• Bitcoin World\
• Ethereum World\
• XRP World\
• Solana World

World-specific files may define unique mechanics, symbolism, and
entities.

However, they must always comply with:

• CANON_INVARIANTS.md\
• UNIVERSAL_RULES.md

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 6. Entity Authority

Entity files define specific characters, objects, or symbolic elements
within a world.

Entities must obey:

• world rules\
• world overview doctrine\
• universal rules\
• canon invariants

Entities cannot redefine higher-level doctrine.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 7. Technology Neutrality

The HDL canon is **technology-independent**.

The universe may be rendered using:

• Three.js\
• Blender\
• WebXR\
• Unreal Engine\
• future rendering systems

These tools implement the universe.

They do not define it.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 8. Canon Preservation

The purpose of canon authority is to preserve the **coherence and
identity of the HDL universe** as it grows.

The HDL World Engine may expand to include:

• new blockchain worlds\
• new simulations\
• new environments\
• new visual systems

However, expansion must never break the underlying doctrine.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Closing Principle

Canon is the **constitution of the HDL universe**.

Tools may change.

Worlds may expand.

Technology will evolve.

But the authority of canon remains.
