# Product Principles

## Purpose
This document converts the broader Hodlneer vision, manifesto, PRD, and identity model into a short set of hard guardrails that future teams can use in day-to-day decision making. It answers: what to optimize for, what to refuse, how to resolve ambiguity, and how the product stays coherent as more people touch it.

This is not a philosophical essay. This is the operational guardrail layer.

See also: [[HODLNEER_INDEX]] | [[CANON_INDEX]] | [[IDENTITY_MODEL]] | [[MANIFESTO]]

## Hodlneer Product Principles

### 1. Proof Over Claim
When there is tension between what a user says and what can be credibly supported, the product must favor proof.

**Why:** Hodlneer exists to bring credibility to crypto identity. If proof becomes optional in spirit, the entire category collapses into aesthetics.

**Implication:** Verification strength must always matter. Proof logic must not be diluted for convenience. Badge prestige must remain tied to evidence, not vibes.

---

### 2. Era and Verification Must Stay Separate
Hodlneer must never collapse historical placement and proof strength into one muddy status tier.

**Why:** A user can be early and weakly proven. A user can be later and strongly proven. The system only feels fair if both truths remain visible.

**Implication:** Identity outputs should use `Era • Verification`. Visual systems must preserve both dimensions. No team should "simplify" this into generic tier soup.

---

### 3. Privacy Over Forced Exposure
Users should be able to carry credible identity without unnecessarily exposing raw source data.

**Why:** Crypto identity should not require financial or forensic self-doxxing just to feel real.

**Implication:** Badge result may be public. Raw source evidence should remain private by default. Balances, wallet details, and proof artifacts should not leak. Integration with HDL or future surfaces must respect this boundary.

---

### 4. Credibility Must Remain Public if the Badge Is Public
If a user displays a Hodlneer badge, the badge's meaning must remain legible.

**Why:** If users can hide or rewrite the credibility layer while keeping the prestige object, trust breaks.

**Implication:** Era and verification classifications must remain public when the badge is shown. Teams must not create prestige display modes that remove badge meaning.

---

### 5. The Badge Is the Hero Object
The badge is not a decorative icon, a secondary embellishment, or a minor UI element. The badge is the emotional, visual, and symbolic center of the product.

**Why:** The product is not just verification logic. It is proof made visible.

**Implication:** The badge must be treated as a premium artifact. The card is the stage, not the hero. HDL surfaces must not flatten it into a forgettable icon. Wallpaper and sharing should preserve its dignity.

---

### 6. Reveal Over Display
Identity should not feel dumped onto the screen. It should feel revealed.

**Why:** If identity matters, it should feel significant. The reveal is part of the meaning, not ornamental fluff.

**Implication:** The reveal sequence is a signature feature. "Success screen" thinking is not acceptable. Motion, audio, and pacing should support emotional legitimacy. Reduced motion versions must still preserve dignity.

---

### 7. Standalone Product First
Hodlneer must always deliver complete standalone value.

**Why:** If Hodlneer becomes merely a lead funnel into HDL.fun, the product loses integrity.

**Implication:** Users must be able to onboard, verify, reveal, wallpaper, and share without HDL. HDL should be framed as an expansion layer, not a justification layer. Future planning must preserve independent product gravity.

---

### 8. HDL Integration Must Be Additive, Not Dominant
The HDL relationship should strengthen Hodlneer without swallowing it.

**Why:** The ecosystem works best when Hodlneer proves identity and HDL expands where identity can live. Reversing that would muddy both products.

**Implication:** Cross-product prompts must appear after standalone value is delivered. HDL must not require access to private proof data by default. Bridge copy should feel like a next layer, not a forced handoff.

---

### 9. Design Must Carry Meaning, Not Just Polish
Every major visual choice in Hodlneer should mean something.

**Why:** This product is too identity-heavy and culturally loaded to survive on surface gloss alone.

**Implication:** The hexagon must mean identity cell, not random style. The badge must connect to the Bitcoin/world visualization system (see [[BADGE_TO_BITCOIN_WORLD_VISUAL_SYSTEM]]). No "cool for cool's sake" design drift.

---

### 10. The Product Must Feel Premium Without Becoming Tacky
Hodlneer should feel prestigious, collectible, expensive, and world-class. But it must never become gaudy, noisy, meme-cheap, loot-box cringe, or faux-luxury nonsense.

**Why:** Crypto already has enough ugly flex culture. Hodlneer should feel elevated.

**Implication:** Motion must stay controlled. Materials must feel refined. Copy must stay confident, not desperate. Social sharing must feel sharp, not spammy.

---

### 11. Dignity for All User Types
The system should create room for early users, later users, private users, loud users, highly verified users, and lightly verified users — without becoming elitist sludge or meaningless participation theater.

**Why:** The product fails if it only serves OG peacocks.

**Implication:** Later users must still feel valid. Privacy must remain respectable. Verification should matter without humiliating weaker-proof users.

---

### 12. The World and the Product Must Speak One Visual Language
The badge, the card, the reveal, the wallpaper, the Bitcoin sphere, and future 3D/VR spaces must feel like they belong to the same universe.

**Why:** Fragmented visual systems make the whole ecosystem feel fake and improvised.

**Implication:** Teams working on worldbuilding and product surfaces must reference [[BADGE_TO_BITCOIN_WORLD_VISUAL_SYSTEM]]. The badge should feel like one cell from a larger network organism.

---

### 13. Meaningful Simplicity Beats Clever Complexity
When in doubt, choose the version that is easier to understand, easier to explain, harder to misinterpret, and more emotionally direct.

**Why:** This product already has enough depth. It does not need confusion layered on top.

**Implication:** `Era • Verification` is better than tier soup. One strong reveal moment is better than too many gimmicks. Clear public/private rules beat clever edge-case exceptions.

---

### 14. Product Truth Must Survive Every Surface
Whether a user sees Hodlneer in the app, a share card, a wallpaper, HDL.fun, a 3D world, or a future VR scene, the same core truths should survive: proof matters, era and verification matter, the badge is meaningful, privacy is respected, the object belongs to a larger world.

**Why:** Consistency is how products become real.

---

### 15. No Team Gets to Freestyle Canon
As more people touch this product, improvisation becomes a liability.

**Why:** Without hard guardrails, teams will simplify the wrong thing, overdesign the wrong thing, contradict each other.

**Implication:** Canon docs must be referenced before major product/design decisions. If a new idea conflicts with canon, canon must be intentionally revised — not silently bypassed. Old conversations do not outrank the latest canon docs.

---

## Decision Filter

When a future team is unsure about a feature, flow, or design direction, ask:

1. Does this strengthen proof, or weaken it?
2. Does this preserve the distinction between era and verification?
3. Does this respect privacy without making the badge meaningless?
4. Does this make the badge feel more like a real artifact?
5. Does this keep Hodlneer valuable on its own?
6. Does this preserve a coherent visual/world language?
7. Does this feel premium without feeling tacky?
8. Does this help the user feel proud, not just processed?

If the answer to most of these is "no," the idea probably sucks.
