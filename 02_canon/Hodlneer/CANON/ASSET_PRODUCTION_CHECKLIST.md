# Asset Production Checklist

## Purpose
This document defines the asset production checklist for the standalone Hodlneer product vertical. It turns the canon, launch strategy, screenshot plan, and App Store work into a practical production checklist so that launch execution does not become chaotic, incomplete, or inconsistent.

This is the production discipline layer — not just a list of files.

See also: [[HODLNEER_INDEX]] | [[CANON_INDEX]] | [[LAUNCH_STRATEGY]] | [[EPICS]]

---

## Core Production Principle

> Hodlneer should launch like a product that knows exactly what it is.

Every launch-facing asset should reinforce the same truths: proof, badge desirability, reveal payoff, wallpaper value, privacy confidence, optional HDL expansion.

If the asset stack tells five different stories, launch quality collapses.

---

## Asset Priority Levels

- **Tier 1 — Must Exist Before Public Launch** (mandatory)
- **Tier 2 — Strongly Recommended** (materially improve clarity, conversion, or trust)
- **Tier 3 — Nice to Have** (can follow after launch if necessary)

---

# Tier 1 — Must Exist Before Public Launch

## 1. Final Product Positioning One-Liner
**Purpose:** Ensure every team can answer what Hodlneer is in one sentence.

**Required Outcome:** A single approved line, e.g.: *Hodlneer is the iOS app for proving your crypto origin and turning it into a badge worth carrying.*

**Owner(s):** product, copy, founder/brand lead

**Status Rule:** No launch without this being locked.

---

## 2. Landing Page Copy
**Purpose:** Public-facing explanation and conversion surface.

**Required Outcome:** Hero section, product explanation, Era × Verification explanation, wallpaper explanation, privacy explanation, HDL connection explanation, CTA structure.

**Owner(s):** copy, product marketing, web design

---

## 3. App Store Page Copy
**Purpose:** iOS-native conversion and App Store clarity.

**Required Outcome:** App name, subtitle, promotional text, long description, feature bullets, screenshot copy alignment.

**Owner(s):** copy, product, launch lead

---

## 4. App Icon
**Purpose:** The first visual handshake on the device and in the App Store.

**Required Outcome:** Approved app icon, small-size legibility validation, alignment with [[ICON_SYSTEM]] canon.

**Owner(s):** design, brand lead, product approval

---

## 5. Badge Hero Renders
**Purpose:** Core visual asset showing the badge as a premium artifact.

**Required Outcome:** High-quality hero badge renders, at least one reveal-end pose, at least one neutral premium badge state, render-ready for landing page, App Store, social, and launch posts.

**Owner(s):** 3D / motion / design

---

## 6. Screenshot Set
**Purpose:** App Store and launch conversion frames.

**Required Outcome:** Screenshots aligned to [[SCREENSHOT_STORYBOARD]], clean typography, final approved export set, ready for App Store submission and social reuse.

**Owner(s):** product design, marketing design, launch lead

---

## 7. Reveal Teaser Clip
**Purpose:** Show the product's emotional differentiator in motion.

**Required Outcome:** Short teaser clip or reveal preview, usable for launch posts and landing page support, aligned to [[REVEAL_SEQUENCE_SPEC]] canon.

**Owner(s):** motion, 3D, launch creative

---

## 8. Wallpaper Showcase Assets
**Purpose:** Prove the product is genuinely iPhone-native and not just talking a big game.

**Required Outcome:** Lock screen showcase, home screen showcase, at least one premium wallpaper mockup, ready for App Store, landing page, and social use.

**Owner(s):** design, 3D/motion, launch creative

---

## 9. Launch Announcement Copy
**Purpose:** Actual public-facing launch language.

**Required Outcome:** Primary launch statement, short post versions, launch thread opener, founder statement variant, email version if applicable.

**Owner(s):** copy, founder/brand lead

See [[LAUNCH_ANNOUNCEMENT]].

---

## 10. Privacy Explanation Snippet
**Purpose:** Answer the trust question quickly and cleanly.

**Required Outcome:** A short, reusable explanation of what is public, what is private, and how the badge works without oversharing.

**Owner(s):** copy, product, trust/privacy reviewer

---

## 11. Era × Verification Explainer
**Purpose:** Make the core logic understandable without a lecture.

**Required Outcome:** A short marketing-use explanation, a slightly fuller FAQ/support version, screenshot and landing page consistency.

**Owner(s):** product, copy

---

## 12. HDL Bridge Copy
**Purpose:** Prevent sloppy ecosystem messaging.

**Required Outcome:** Short explanation of how Hodlneer connects to HDL.fun, clear optional framing, no dependency confusion.

**Owner(s):** product, copy, ecosystem lead

# Production Workstreams

**Copy Lane** owns: landing page, App Store copy, announcement copy, FAQ, HDL bridge messaging.

**Visual Design Lane** owns: screenshot system, app icon, badge hero renders, wallpaper presentations, supporting launch graphics.

**Motion / 3D Lane** owns: reveal teaser, app preview/trailer, badge motion outputs, wallpaper motion or showcase assets.

**Product / Canon Lane** owns: identity model alignment, privacy clarity, message hierarchy enforcement, approval against canon.

**Ecosystem Lane** owns: HDL integration presentation, connected visuals, cross-promo alignment.

---

# Approval Logic

Every core asset should pass these checks before approval.

**Product Truth Check:** Does it reinforce proof, badge, wallpaper, privacy, and optional HDL next layer?

**Canon Check:** Does it align with identity model, product principles, badge visual system, and launch strategy?

**Emotional Check:** Does it feel premium, clear, and worth carrying?

**Anti-Bullshit Check:** Does it avoid crypto cringe, overhype, fake luxury, HDL confusion, and generic app-store blandness?
