# Icon System

## Purpose
This document defines the icon system for the standalone Hodlneer product vertical. It ensures that Hodlneer's iconography — from the app icon to supporting glyphs and badge-adjacent symbols — is coherent, recognizable, and grounded in the same visual logic as the badge, reveal, wallpaper, and world visualization systems.

See also: [[HODLNEER_INDEX]] | [[CANON_INDEX]] | [[BADGE_VISUAL_SYSTEM]] | [[BADGE_TO_BITCOIN_WORLD_VISUAL_SYSTEM]]

---

## Core Icon Principle

> Every Hodlneer icon should feel like a reduced, disciplined expression of the same world the badge comes from.

The icon system is not separate from the badge system. The app icon is not the badge itself, but it must feel related. Supporting glyphs should inherit the same geometry logic and design discipline. Icons should feel intentional, not generic.

---

## Icon System Layers

### 1. App Icon
The primary iOS product mark seen on the home screen, App Store, and device surfaces.

### 2. Product Mark / Core Symbol
A simplified emblematic symbol for tighter contexts: launch graphics, loading states, subtle branding moments, watermark or stamp usage, HDL integration references.

### 3. Supporting UI Glyphs
A supporting set of smaller icons used in: verification flows, settings, reveal interface moments, privacy controls, badge detail screens.

All three layers must feel related without becoming repetitive clones of each other.

---

## App Icon Strategy

The app icon should **not** simply paste the full badge onto the iPhone home screen. That would be lazy and visually muddy.

The app icon should feel like: a compressed expression of the badge system, a premium symbol of proof-based identity, unmistakably tied to the hex-based design universe, strong enough to hold up at small sizes, elegant enough to survive App Store presentation.

### The app icon should communicate
Precision, identity, premium design, world coherence, strength through geometry.

### The app icon should not communicate
"Just another crypto app," "generic web3 logo," "coin/token icon," "random futuristic hex because crypto," "sports team shield."

---

## Relationship Between App Icon and Badge

**Important Rule:** The app icon is **not** the full badge. The app icon is a distilled symbol derived from the same visual logic.

**Why:** The full badge is richer and more dimensional. The app icon must be recognizable at tiny sizes. The icon needs simpler massing and higher contrast. The icon should hint at the artifact, not flatten it.

**Best Mental Model:** The badge is the artifact. The app icon is the seal.

---

## Core Geometry Rule

The icon system must inherit the same fundamental geometry family as the badge and world: hex-based structural logic, disciplined symmetry, reduced network/cell language, a sense of refined structural segmentation, premium restraint.

### Strong Recommendation
The core mark should likely emerge from: one dominant hex-based form, one internal cut, seam, or nucleus cue, and one memorable structural distinction. This keeps the mark iconic instead of busy.

---

## Shape Language

The icon system should feel: faceted, engineered, precise, compact, elegant, premium.

It should not feel: soft and bubbly, tribal, ornamental, overly spiky, whimsical, meme-y.

---

## App Icon Visual Guidance

### Desired Traits
- strong silhouette at small size
- immediate recognizability
- deep but clean visual layering
- premium depth or lighting treatment if appropriate
- feels native to iOS while still distinct

### Possible Directions
- distilled central hex nucleus
- layered hex seal
- abstracted identity-cell core
- minimal artifact fragment

### Warning
Do not overfit the app icon to: tiny text, excessive detail, overly literal badge rendering, fake metallic over-rendering. If the icon only looks good in a giant mockup and collapses at 60px, it failed.

---

## Product Mark / Core Symbol

In addition to the app icon, Hodlneer should have a simplified product mark usable in: launch graphics, loading/splash contexts, small branding references, watermark/stamp usage, future HDL integration surfaces.

### The product mark should be
Simpler than the badge, possibly simpler than the full app icon, usable in monochrome, legible in small branding contexts, structurally aligned with the badge and icon system.

### The product mark is not
A random "H," a typographic hack with no world meaning, or a totally separate logo language. It must still feel born from the same geometry.

---

## Supporting UI Glyph System

The smaller UI icons inside Hodlneer should feel like they belong. This does not mean every settings icon needs to look like sci-fi sculpture — but line weight, corner logic, geometry discipline, and emphasis style should feel like one system.

### Supporting glyphs should feel
Clean, premium, slightly engineered, modern, legible first and stylized second.

### Supporting glyphs should not feel
Cartoonish, default system-icon leftovers everywhere, mismatched with the badge/artifact world, or over-custom to the point of hurting usability.

Use discipline, not novelty addiction.

---

## Icon System and the Hex Identity Cell

Because the badge is the personal identity cell and the broader world is built from many such cells, the icon system should subtly reinforce this. The app icon can hint at identity-cell logic. Supporting symbols can borrow from segmented or nested structure. Hints are stronger than shouting.

---

## Color and Material Guidance

### App Icon
May use: premium contrast, luminous detail, restrained depth, a dark-luxury or high-contrast base.

Should avoid: rainbow crypto gradients, noisy neon overload, generic gold-coin clichés, childish saturation.

### Product Mark
Should also survive: monochrome, limited-color, emboss or stamp-like use, reduced branding contexts. If the mark only works in one over-rendered shiny mode, that is a weakness.

---

## App Icon Evaluation Questions

Before approving any Hodlneer app icon, ask:

1. Does it still look strong at iPhone home-screen size?
2. Does it feel related to the badge system without duplicating the badge literally?
3. Does it feel premium without becoming tacky?
4. Does it feel like crypto identity, not just generic crypto?
5. Does it belong in the same world as the badge and sphere visualization?
6. Would someone recognize it again after seeing it once or twice?

---

## Relationship to HDL.fun

The Hodlneer icon system must remain distinct from HDL.fun's identity system. Do not visually merge the brands or make Hodlneer feel like an HDL sub-brand icon.

But the two should not feel like they come from alien civilizations either. They should feel related at the ecosystem level while preserving product-specific identity.

---

## Final Canonical Statement

> The Hodlneer icon system should feel like a distilled seal of the badge world — recognizable, premium, and born from the same geometry, without collapsing into generic crypto branding.
