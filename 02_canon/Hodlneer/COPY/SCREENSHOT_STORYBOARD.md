# Screenshot Storyboard

## Purpose
This document defines the screenshot storyboard for the standalone Hodlneer iOS app. It turns the product strategy, App Store page, reveal system, wallpaper system, and launch narrative into a concrete visual sequence that designers, motion teams, and launch staff can execute without guessing.

This is the visual conversion sequence for launch.

See also: [[HODLNEER_INDEX]] | [[APP_STORE_PAGE]] | [[WALLPAPER_SYSTEM]] | [[LAUNCH_STRATEGY]]

---

## Core Screenshot Principle

> The screenshot sequence should sell Hodlneer as a premium identity artifact product in under ten seconds.

The sequence must communicate, fast: 1) proof, 2) badge desirability, 3) reveal energy, 4) wallpaper payoff, 5) privacy intelligence, 6) optional HDL connection.

---

## General Screenshot Rules

### The screenshots must feel
Premium, cinematic, iPhone-native, emotionally clear, minimal but meaningful, badge-first.

### The screenshots must not feel like
SaaS panels, cluttered dashboards, crypto spreadsheets, generic onboarding screens, HDL.fun promo slides, or overdesigned poster art with unreadable UI.

### Important Production Rule
These should not be simple raw screenshots with text slapped on top later. They should be intentionally composed launch frames.

---

## Recommended Screenshot Count

**Recommended Baseline:** 6 screenshots (enough to tell the full conversion story without dilution)

**Optional Extended:** Up to 8 screenshots for stronger HDL bridge explanation, additional privacy detail, alternate wallpaper showcase, or localization needs.

# Screenshot Storyboard

## Screenshot 1 — Category Hook

**Strategic Job:** Immediately tell the user what Hodlneer is about.

**Headline:** Prove your crypto origin

**Subheadline:** A premium badge built from history and proof.

**Emotional Goal:** Intrigue, clarity, seriousness.

**Visual Composition:** Bold hero badge or reveal-end badge state, dark premium background, strong silhouette, very limited UI clutter, phone frame feels elegant and immediate.

**Notes:** This is the money shot. If screenshot one is weak, the whole sequence limps.

---

## Screenshot 2 — Reveal Moment

**Strategic Job:** Show that this is not just a static badge app. The identity is revealed.

**Headline:** Your identity is revealed

**Subheadline:** Not assigned. Revealed through a cinematic badge experience.

**Emotional Goal:** Anticipation, product differentiation, premium excitement.

**Visual Composition:** Mid-reveal or reveal impact frame, visible motion energy or post-impact lighting, badge clearly central, controlled brightness burst or reveal atmosphere, enough UI context to suggest this is in-app, not pure poster art.

**Notes:** This should make people think: *"Okay, that's actually sick."*

---

## Screenshot 3 — Era × Verification Logic

**Strategic Job:** Explain the core identity system simply and fast.

**Headline:** Based on era and proof

**Subheadline:** Your badge reflects when you entered crypto and how strongly it can be verified.

**Emotional Goal:** Trust, fairness, understanding.

**Visual Composition:** Badge detail screen or elegant identity detail view, the `Era • Verification` structure clearly visible, no jargon overload, visually clean enough to understand in two seconds.

**Notes:** This is where the product earns legitimacy. Do not overcomplicate this frame.

---

## Screenshot 4 — Wallpaper Payoff

**Strategic Job:** Show the strongest iPhone-native differentiator.

**Headline:** Turn it into your wallpaper

**Subheadline:** Carry your badge on your iPhone and show it off without saying a word.

**Emotional Goal:** Desire, ownership, lifestyle connection.

**Visual Composition:** Lock screen or home screen display, badge living naturally as wallpaper, unmistakably iPhone-native, premium composition with breathing room around clock/icons, badge remains hero.

**Notes:** This screenshot should make people imagine it on their own phone. That is the point.

---

## Screenshot 5 — Privacy Intelligence

**Strategic Job:** Reassure users that the system is proof-based without requiring oversharing.

**Headline:** Show the badge. Keep the details private.

**Subheadline:** Your identity can be credible without exposing everything behind it.

**Emotional Goal:** Trust, modern privacy confidence, emotional safety.

**Visual Composition:** Badge detail/privacy settings state, calm minimal UI, visual emphasis on controlled visibility, no cluttered settings wall, elegant — not defensive.

**Notes:** This removes a silent objection before the user even articulates it.

---

## Screenshot 6 — HDL Bridge

**Strategic Job:** Show that Hodlneer stands alone, but can connect into a richer ecosystem later.

**Headline:** Bring it into HDL.fun

**Subheadline:** Use your badge inside a larger wallet and social world when you want the next layer.

**Emotional Goal:** Expansion, optional future value, ecosystem intrigue.

**Visual Composition:** Tasteful integration moment, Hodlneer badge shown inside a richer HDL identity surface, HDL appears as the next layer, not the main character, feels additive not dependency-driven.

**Notes:** This frame belongs last on purpose. HDL should enter after Hodlneer already makes sense on its own.

# Screenshot Order Logic

The sequence follows this user psychology:
1. What is this?
2. Why is it different?
3. Why is it legitimate?
4. Why would I want it on my phone?
5. Will this force me to expose too much?
6. What else can it become?

Do not shuffle the order casually.

---

# Visual Production Guidance

### Every screenshot should preserve
Badge-first hierarchy, premium dark-luxury or high-contrast feel, clean copy, readable typography, enough UI to show product reality, enough composition to feel like a campaign asset.

### Typography Guidance
Headline copy: bold, short, high-contrast, emotionally direct.
Subheadline copy: shorter than landing page language, easier than manifesto language, sharper than generic marketing fluff.

### Motion-to-Still Guidance
Choose stills carefully: post-impact reveal freeze, badge in stable premium idle state, wallpaper at its strongest composed moment, privacy screen with meaningful but uncluttered controls. Avoid: blurry transition moments, empty loader frames, tiny unreadable UI states.
