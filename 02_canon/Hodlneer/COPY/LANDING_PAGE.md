# Landing Page Copy

## Purpose
This document defines the landing page copy, structure, and messaging hierarchy for the standalone Hodlneer product vertical. The landing page presents Hodlneer as a standalone identity product, a premium digital badge experience, a privacy-first crypto origin verification system, a collectible and shareable 3D wallpaper app, and a product that can optionally connect into HDL.fun.

See also: [[HODLNEER_INDEX]] | [[MANIFESTO]] | [[APP_STORE_PAGE]] | [[LAUNCH_STRATEGY]]

---

## Landing Page Strategy

The landing page must accomplish five jobs:

1. Explain what Hodlneer is in emotionally clear language
2. Make the badge feel prestigious and collectible
3. Explain privacy without sounding defensive
4. Introduce the 3D wallpaper concept as a key differentiator
5. Position HDL.fun as an optional unlock, not a requirement

This page should make a visitor feel: intrigued, impressed, understood, curious to verify themselves, eager to see the reveal.

## Messaging Hierarchy

1. Identity
2. Proof
3. Artifact
4. Privacy
5. Sharing
6. HDL Integration

**Do not lead with HDL.fun.**

## FAQ Copy Suggestions

**What determines my badge?**
Your badge is based on your crypto era and the strength of the verification behind it.

**Do I have to reveal my wallet or balances publicly?**
No. Hodlneer is built to verify identity without forcing unnecessary public exposure.

**Is Hodlneer the same as HDL.fun?**
No. Hodlneer is a standalone identity and badge app. HDL.fun is a separate but connected wallet and social ecosystem.

**Can I use Hodlneer without HDL.fun?**
Yes. Hodlneer is designed to stand on its own.

**Why make this its own app?**
Because this experience needs its own lane, its own onboarding, and its own product identity. The badge is not just a feature. It is the product.

---

## Tone Notes

The landing page tone should be: confident, premium, cinematic, clear, emotionally precise.

Avoid: overexplaining crypto jargon, sounding like a meme coin page, sounding like a technical spec, sounding too corporate or sterile, making non-OG users feel dismissed.

---

## Final Canonical Statement

> The landing page should make Hodlneer feel like a premium iPhone-native identity product, not a technical crypto utility.
